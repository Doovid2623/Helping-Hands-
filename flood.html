Flood and Safety Tips

Flood

Floods are the most common natural disaster and occur when too much overflows onto land that is usually dry. Flood are often caused by heavy rainfall, rapid melting snow, or a storm surge from a tropical cyclone or tsunami in coastal areas. From 1998 to 2017, flood affected more than 2 billion people worldwide. People are most at risk if they live in areas where floods often happen, in weak buildings, or if they don't have warning systems or know how to stay safe during floods.

Safety Tips For Flood

  • Secure or protect securing anything that could be dangerous.
  • Turn of electrical power, gas and water supplies before you leave.
  • Avoid low spots, flooded areas or walking through water.
  • Listen to local radio or TV for updates.
  • Avoid driving into flooded areas.

Causes

Heavy Rainfall

Clouds are made of water droplets. Within a cloud, water droplets condense onto one another, causing the droplets to grow. When these water droplets get too heavy to stay suspended in the cloud, they fall to Earth as rain.

Ocean Waves

Storm surge when water rises higher than normal during a big tropical storm. When the water rises, this can cause dangerous flooding in coastal areas.

Melting snow and ice

An ice jam happens when pieces of ice pile up and block a river. This usually happens in spring when snow and ice start to melt.

Prevention

Monitor local weather and alerts

Stay updated by regularly monitoring local weather reports and emergency alerts to be aware of any potential flood risks in your area. Make sure to listen to the news, check reliable weather apps, or follow official sources for timely flood warnings.

Avoid building in floodplains

Avoid constructing homes or buildings in flood-prone areas, as these locations are highly susceptible to flooding. If building in such areas is necessary, ensure that your home is properly elevated and reinforced with flood-resistant materials to minimize potential damage.

Construct barriers

Building protective barriers around your home before a flood occurs is an effective way to prevent water from entering and causing damage. Flood barriers, such as levees, sandbags, or flood walls, help block or slow down rising water levels, keeping your property safer.

Extend downspouts away

Downspout extenders are useful for directing rainwater away from your home’s foundation, preventing it from accumulating near the base of your house. By improving drainage, these extenders help reduce the risk of basement flooding, water damage, and soil erosion.
